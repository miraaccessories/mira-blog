You've been invited to a baby shower for someone you know from work, or a friend of a friend, or a cousin you see once a year. You want to give something appropriate and appreciated without making it awkward. This situation comes up constantly in South African social life. Here's how to navigate it well.

Go Universal First

Gifts that work regardless of taste, nursery theme, or personal style are your best and safest option when you don't know the recipient well. Neutral colours, classic styles, things that are useful regardless of individual preference. Hair accessories in soft neutral tones — ivory, lilac, blush, sage — are universally flattering and work with any nursery colour scheme, any aesthetic philosophy, and any personal style.

Avoid Theme-Specific Items

Unless you know the nursery theme and know the mom loves it, avoid items that only work within a specific aesthetic. A gift that only works if she's doing a safari theme is a gift that creates a small problem if she isn't. Universal beats specific every time when you're buying for someone you don't know well.

Let Presentation Do the Work

When you don't have personal knowledge of the recipient, presentation communicates care and consideration. A beautiful, well-packaged gift says you put thought into this — even if the thought was primarily about finding something lovely that would work for anyone. Presentation is the personalisation when personalisation isn't possible.

Spend Appropriately for the Relationship

For a colleague or acquaintance, R150 to R350 is the right range. You don't need to spend more to give a great gift — thoughtfulness, presentation, and utility matter more than price. Within this budget, a well-chosen gift that arrives beautifully presented will always be appreciated and remembered positively.

Write Something Real in the Card

When you don't know someone well, the card is your opportunity to make the gift personal. Write something genuine — a wish for the baby and the new family, a welcome to parenthood, something that shows you thought about this person specifically even if you don't know her well. It transforms a well-chosen generic gift into something remembered.
