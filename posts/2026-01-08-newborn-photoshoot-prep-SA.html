The newborn photoshoot window is roughly two to three weeks long. After that, the floppy, sleepy, curled-up newborn stage begins to change — babies unfold, become more alert, and the poses that work beautifully at ten days become impossible at six weeks. If you want newborn photos, here's everything you need to know.

The Timing Question

The ideal window for newborn photos is days seven to fourteen after birth. Before day seven, the baby is often still adjusting and may have more jaundice colouring or an unsettled cord stump to work around. After day fourteen, they're less sleepy and the characteristic curled poses become progressively harder to achieve.

This means you need to book your photographer before the baby arrives. Most South African newborn photographers have a provisional booking system — you book a tentative date around your due date and confirm once the baby has arrived.

Studio or At Home?

Both are beautiful and both produce stunning results. A studio shoot gives you professional props, backdrops, controlled lighting, and a photographer experienced in safely positioning a newborn. An at-home shoot is more intimate and personal — your home, your things, the natural light through your windows. Many of the most emotionally resonant newborn photos in South Africa are taken at home precisely because you can see the life the baby is arriving into.

What to Wear

Neutral tones photograph best in almost every lighting condition. White, cream, blush, grey, sage, dusty blue. Bold colours can compete with the baby and create images that feel busy. If you want colour in the image, introduce it through the accessory rather than the clothing — a neutral outfit with a beautiful blush or lilac hair accessory gives you the colour without the risk.

The Baby's Schedule on Shoot Day

A fed, sleepy baby is a cooperative baby. Plan the shoot for just after a feeding. A warm room — warmer than you'd normally keep it, because sleepy babies prefer warmth — and white noise in the background helps keep newborns settled throughout the session. Brief breaks for feeding, comforting, and settling are completely normal and any professional newborn photographer will expect and accommodate them.

What to Tell Your Photographer

Tell them which poses matter most to you. Whether you want to be in the photos or prefer just-baby shots. Any items with sentimental meaning you'd like included. Any physical considerations from the birth that they should know about. A good photographer wants this information — it shapes the session and ensures you get the images that matter most to you.

SA Mom Tip

After the shoot, print the photos. Put them on the wall. In the rush of new parenthood, physical photos don't always get prioritised, and the newborn stage is the one you'll most want to be able to look at. Digital files on a hard drive are invisible. A framed photo in your home is something you see every day.
