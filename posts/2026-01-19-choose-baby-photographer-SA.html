Your baby's photos are forever. The images you have from this stage will be looked at for the rest of your life. The photographer you choose makes a significant difference to what you'll have — and what you'll feel when you look at it thirty years from now. It's worth taking the time to choose well.

Start with the Portfolio

A photographer's portfolio tells you almost everything you need to know before you speak to them. Look at it carefully and ask yourself: is the light consistent and beautiful across different sessions? Skilled photographers control light deliberately. Are the babies comfortable-looking? Poses that look forced or strained usually are. Does the editing style match what you want? Does the style itself — posed and polished, or documentary and natural — match your vision?

Consistent lighting, comfortable-looking subjects, and a clear aesthetic vision are the three things that distinguish an experienced baby photographer from someone still developing their eye.

Key Questions to Ask Before Booking

Ask how many newborn sessions they've completed — experience matters significantly in newborn photography, where safety and patience are paramount. Ask how long a session typically takes — a good newborn session can run two to four hours, and a photographer who quotes you sixty minutes may be cutting corners. Ask about their reschedule policy if your baby is unwell on the shoot day. And ask when you can expect to receive your edited images — two to four weeks is standard.

Safety Note

Some newborn poses that circulate widely on social media are not safely achievable without specific training and an assistant. A responsible, trained newborn photographer knows which poses require composite editing (multiple safe shots combined), and will never put a baby in an unsafe position. Ask about their safety training and process if this concerns you.

Red Flags

Prices that seem too low are almost always a signal. Professional newborn photography requires equipment, insurance, significant time, and ongoing training. A price that seems dramatically lower than competitors usually reflects shortcuts somewhere. Forced or uncomfortable-looking poses in the portfolio are a concern. No consultation or pre-session communication is unusual for a professional — experienced photographers want to know about your baby and your vision before the day.

Supporting Local

South Africa has exceptional baby photographers across every province. Supporting local photographers — especially those working in your city or area — builds the local creative economy and often produces more personalised, attentive service than a large photography operation. Ask for recommendations in your local parenting community — word of mouth from people who have actually used a photographer is invaluable.
