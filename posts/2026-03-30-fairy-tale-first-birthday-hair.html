The first birthday only happens once. It's also the birthday that will be photographed the most thoroughly of any birthday your child has — and the photos will be looked at for the rest of your lives. Here's how to create first birthday hair that's beautiful, comfortable, and unforgettable in those photos.

The First Birthday Hair Challenge

Most one-year-olds have varying amounts of hair — from very little to a reasonable amount, depending on the child. The hairstyle needs to work with whatever hair is actually there. It also needs to be comfortable enough for a baby who will be worn, carried, put down, picked up, fed cake, and photographed from every angle for several hours. This is not the day for uncomfortable styling.

The Simple Bow — The Best Choice

For one-year-olds with limited hair: a single beautiful clip or bow placed deliberately where it shows in photos. If there's a crown section with enough hair to grip, clip it there. If not, a soft headband with a beautiful bow or flower attached. The accessory does the work of making the look feel special when the hair itself can't.

Coordinate With the Birthday Outfit

The first birthday dress or outfit is usually chosen first. Build the hair accessory choice around it — same colour family, or deliberately contrasting in a considered way. The accessory and the outfit together create the first birthday look that will appear in every photo from the day.

The Practical Note

Have a backup plan. Bring a second accessory in case the first one becomes uncomfortable or falls out during the party. First birthdays are long events and babies' patience for accessories — like all their patience — is variable. A quick switch to a softer headband if the clip becomes an issue is a five-second fix that means the rest of the day's photos still look beautiful.
