You can have too many and spend every morning hunting through a tangled drawer. You can have too few and be caught on school photo day with nothing clean. Here's the practical answer to exactly how many hair accessories a South African toddler needs — and what they should be.

The Minimum Functional Collection

For a toddler attending school or crèche, the minimum functional collection is: six everyday clips or bows (to allow for washing and drying between uses, plus the occasional lost one), one or two special occasion clips, a set of soft fabric elastics (keep a full pack), and one or two soft headbands for variety. This is not many items. It's enough to cover every situation a toddler encounters in a school term without becoming overwhelming.

Why Six Everyday Clips

One in use. One drying after washing. Two available backups. One in her school bag. One that's inexplicably somewhere else entirely. Six covers the reality of toddler hair accessory logistics without requiring constant shopping or washing. If you have fewer than four, you will run into problems. If you have more than eight, you'll have too many to organise effectively.

What You Don't Need

Seventeen different novelty clips in specific themes that only work with one outfit. Clips that are so large they're impractical for a school day. Multiple versions of the same colour clip when one or two would suffice. More headbands than she can wear in a fortnight. The goal is a curated, functional collection — not a collection that becomes a storage problem.

When to Add More

Add to the collection when something is genuinely worn out or lost and needs replacing. When she's entering a new season of life — school, Grade R, a change in hair length — that requires different accessories. And for special occasions that warrant a more beautiful accessory than the everyday collection includes. That's it. Resist the impulse to buy more simply because something is pretty — prettiness alone is not a reason to grow the collection beyond what's functional.
